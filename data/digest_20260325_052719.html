Prices vs. Quantities: Robust Regulation
arxiv • Zi Yang Kang • 2026-03-16
Score: 11 mechanism design, screening
This paper examines the classic prices vs. quantities instrument choice problem using robust mechanism design, where a regulator facing uncertainty about the correlation between individual demand and marginal externalities chooses policies based on worst-case welfare outcomes. The methodology is theoretical, employing robust optimization techniques within a mechanism design framework. The key finding is that quantity controls (floors for positive externalities, ceilings for negative externalities) are optimal under uncertainty, while uniform taxes/subsidies can be optimal only when the sign of the demand-externality correlation is known, providing a welfare-theoretic rationale for the widespread use of non-price regulatory instruments.
The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper examines why the US and China are accelerating AI investment despite catastrophic AGI risks, contradicting standard real options theory that predicts investment delays under extreme uncertainty. Using a continuous-time preemption game with endogenous existential risk, the authors show that when catastrophic failure costs are shared globally, the risk term cancels out of players' equilibrium conditions, creating a "suicide region" where competitive pressures force rational agents to deploy AGI early despite negative risk-adjusted NPV. The key contribution is demonstrating that only internalizing ruin costs through private liability can restore proper investment incentives and halt the dangerous race dynamics.
Existence of Equilibrium Mechanisms in Generalized Principal-Agent Problems with Interacting Teams
arxiv • Brian Roberson • 2026-02-23
Score: 9 mechanism design, principal-agent
This paper addresses the fundamental question of whether equilibria exist in multi-principal mechanism design games where teams interact strategically and each principal's feasible incentive-compatible mechanisms depend on other principals' choices. The authors develop a novel theoretical approach that tracks both truthful equilibrium outcomes and deviations to handle discontinuities in the incentive-compatibility correspondence that can prevent equilibrium existence (as shown in Myerson 1982). The key contribution is establishing general sufficient conditions for equilibrium existence in these complex environments, providing theoretical foundations for analyzing strategic interactions in team production and multi-agency settings.
A Real-Options-Aware Multi-Criteria Framework for Ex-Ante Real Estate Redevelopment Use Selection
arxiv • Roberto Garrone • 2026-01-15
Score: 9 screening, real options
This paper addresses how to select optimal intended uses for underperforming real estate assets requiring redevelopment by developing a decision framework that treats use selection as a strategic option choice rather than a design optimization problem. The methodology combines real options theory (incorporating irreversibility and managerial flexibility) with multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA) to evaluate alternatives across dimensions of economic value, risk, complexity, and time-to-income. The key contribution is an integrated analytical framework that preserves option value through disciplined ex-ante screening, reducing misalignment between asset use and market demand while avoiding over-complexification in redevelopment decisions.
Stochastic Optimization and Coupling
arxiv • Frank Yang, Kai Hao Yang • 2026-03-12
Score: 7 mechanism design
This paper investigates stochastic optimization problems where a linear functional is maximized over probability measures constrained by integral stochastic orders in arbitrary dimensions. Using theoretical analysis, the authors establish the equivalence of four key properties: closure of test function cones under pointwise minimum, affinity of the value function, convexity properties of solution correspondences, and existence of order-preserving couplings between measure pairs. The main contribution is a unified framework that generalizes Blackwell's theorem on experiment comparison and provides new theoretical foundations for information design, mechanism design, and decision theory through the characterization of integral stochastic orders.
