Principal-agent problems with adverse selection: A stochastic target problem formulation
arxiv • Guillermo Alonso Alvarez, Ibrahim Ekren, Liwei Huang • 2026-05-01
Score: 16 self-selection, screening, adverse selection
This paper examines a principal-agent problem with adverse selection where the principal must offer a single contract rather than a menu of contracts for self-selection. Using stochastic target problem methodology, the authors reformulate the agent's optimization as a stochastic control problem and characterize the "credible domain" of feasible outcomes. The key contribution is showing how to solve the principal's optimization under partial information with state constraints and demonstrating how this framework can also derive the value of traditional screening contracts.
A Truthful Multiunit Profit-Optimal Mechanism for Synthesizing Social Laws
arxiv • Jun Wu, Jian Huang, Chongjun Wang • 2026-05-16
Score: 16 mechanism design, procurement auction, optimal mechanism
This paper addresses Social Law Synthesis (SLS) in strategic multi-agent environments by formulating it as a Bayesian single-parameter procurement auction using Alternating-time Temporal Logic (ATL). The methodology combines theoretical mechanism design with computational approaches, proving a representation lemma for valuations, reducing payment determination to allocation determination in polynomial time, and encoding the FP^NP-complete allocation problem as integer linear programming constraints. The key contribution is the PO-ASL mechanism that achieves truthfulness, individual rationality, and profit-optimality while being computationally tractable through standard ILP solvers.
