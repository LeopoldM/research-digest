Information Design and Mechanism Design: An Integrated Framework
arxiv • Dirk Bergemann, Tibor Heumann, Stephen Morris • 2026-01-24
Score: 18 mechanism design, auction theory, screening
This paper examines how a designer can jointly optimize both information provision and allocation mechanisms in screening environments. Using majorization theory, the authors show that both problems reduce to maximizing linear functionals under majorization constraints—mechanism design chooses allocations majorized by inventory, while information design chooses structures majorized by priors. The key finding is that when jointly optimizing both information and mechanisms, pooling of agent values and their associated allocations is always optimal, providing a unified framework that extends classical auction theory to information design settings.
The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper investigates why the US and China are accelerating AI investment despite acknowledging catastrophic AGI risks, contrary to standard real options theory which predicts delay under extreme uncertainty. Using a continuous-time preemption game with endogenous existential risk, the authors show that when catastrophic failure costs are shared globally across competitors, the risk terms cancel out of equilibrium conditions, creating a "suicide region" where rational actors deploy AGI early despite negative risk-adjusted NPV. The key contribution is demonstrating that competitive pressures can override safety considerations in winner-take-all races with systemic risks, and that only internalizing ruin costs through private liability can restore incentives for delayed, safer deployment.
Dynamic Mechanism Design without Monetary Transfers: A Queueing Theory Approach
arxiv • Zihao Li, Xuandong Chen • 2026-01-28
Score: 9 mechanism design, optimal mechanism
This paper examines how to optimally allocate stochastically arriving goods to stochastically arriving agents with private types when monetary transfers are prohibited but costly type verification is possible. The methodology combines mechanism design theory with queueing theory to characterize steady-state optimal mechanisms. The key finding is that the optimal mechanism takes the form of a dynamic threshold policy where admission and allocation thresholds depend on current queue states, providing a tractable framework for analyzing allocation problems in public programs and organizational settings without monetary instruments.
The Algorithmic Barrier: Quantifying Artificial Frictional Unemployment in Automated Recruitment Systems
arxiv • Ibrahim Denis Fofanah • 2026-01-20
Score: 9 screening, information asymmetry
This paper investigates whether automated recruitment systems using keyword-based screening contribute to artificially prolonged unemployment by creating inefficient job-candidate matching. The authors employ controlled simulations comparing traditional keyword screening with semantic vector-based matching methods, grounded in labor economics and information asymmetry theory. The key finding is that semantic matching substantially improves recall and matching efficiency without sacrificing precision, suggesting that outdated algorithmic screening creates significant economic costs through "artificial frictional unemployment" caused by semantic misinterpretation of candidate qualifications.
A Real-Options-Aware Multi-Criteria Framework for Ex-Ante Real Estate Redevelopment Use Selection
arxiv • Roberto Garrone • 2026-01-15
Score: 9 screening, real options
This study addresses how to select optimal intended uses for underperforming real estate assets requiring redevelopment, treating this as a strategic option selection problem rather than traditional design or financial optimization. The methodology combines real options theory (to account for irreversibility and managerial flexibility) with multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA) to evaluate alternatives across economic value, risk, complexity, and time-to-income dimensions. The key contribution is a decision framework that preserves option value through disciplined ex-ante screening, demonstrating through illustrative cases that this integrated approach reduces over-complexification and use-demand misalignment in redevelopment decisions.
