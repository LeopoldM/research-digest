Truthful Production Uncertainty in Electricity Markets: A Two-Stage Mechanism
arxiv • Shobhit Singhal, Lesia Mitridati, Licio Romao • 2026-04-02
Score: 13 market clearing, electricity market
This paper addresses how electricity markets can better incorporate production uncertainty from renewable sources by designing a mechanism that elicits truthful uncertainty information from generators. The methodology develops a two-stage theoretical mechanism extending Vickrey-Clarke-Groves (VCG) payments, where producers report their full production forecast distributions in day-ahead markets rather than just point forecasts, followed by realized production reporting in real-time. The key contribution is proving this mechanism achieves incentive compatibility and individual rationality while demonstrating through case studies that accounting for uncertainty information in dispatch decisions reduces total system costs compared to current market designs that ignore production uncertainty.
Carbon Regulation and Competition in the European Airline Industry
arxiv • Ertian Chen, Lichao Chen, Lars Nesheim • 2026-03-29
Score: 13 carbon pricing, emissions trading
This paper examines how EU carbon pricing through the Emissions Trading System affects competition and welfare in the European airline industry. The authors employ a two-stage structural model of airline competition estimated on European market share and pricing data, incorporating endogenous route entry, flight frequency, and pricing decisions. Key findings show highly asymmetric impacts: consumer surplus falls up to 25% overall (90% in medium-haul markets), airline profits decline 8-45%, but short-haul markets experience net welfare gains as airlines reallocate capacity toward shorter routes, with carbon revenues ($0.9-3.1B) and social benefits from avoided emissions ($0.5-1.4B) partially offsetting losses.
Lexicographic Robustness and the Efficiency of Optimal Mechanisms
arxiv • Ashwin Kambhampati • 2026-04-07
Score: 11 mechanism design, screening
This paper addresses how to design robust mechanisms when the designer faces uncertainty about the economic environment by proposing a lexicographic refinement of the standard maxmin criterion. The methodology is theoretical, developing the concept of "proper robustness" as the strongest lexicographic refinement and characterizing optimal mechanisms in screening, auction, and public good settings. The key finding is that proper robustness yields ex post efficient mechanisms in screening and auction environments, but in public good provision it identifies specific optimal inefficiencies that become severe as economies grow large.
Prices vs. Quantities: Robust Regulation
arxiv • Zi Yang Kang • 2026-03-16
Score: 11 mechanism design, screening
This paper examines the classic prices versus quantities instrument choice problem under regulatory uncertainty about the correlation between individual demand and marginal externalities, using robust mechanism design where the regulator maximizes worst-case welfare. The methodology is theoretical, employing mechanism design with robust optimization to characterize optimal policies under uncertainty. The key finding is that quantity controls (floors for positive externalities, ceilings for negative externalities) are optimal under uncertainty, while uniform pricing instruments become optimal only when the sign of the demand-externality correlation is known, providing a welfare-theoretic rationale for the widespread use of quantity-based regulations.
The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper examines why the US and China are accelerating AI investment despite acknowledging catastrophic risks from AGI misalignment, which contradicts standard real options theory that predicts investment delay under high uncertainty. The authors develop a continuous-time preemption game with endogenous existential risk, where the failure cost is a globally-shared systemic ruin parameter rather than just sunk investment costs. The key finding is that shared catastrophic risk mathematically cancels out of players' equilibrium conditions, creating a "suicide region" where competitive pressures force rational agents to deploy AGI early despite negative risk-adjusted NPV, and only internalizing ruin costs through private liability can restore optimal delay incentives.
