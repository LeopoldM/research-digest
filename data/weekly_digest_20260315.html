Debt, Growth, and the Carbon Lock-In
openalex • Silvia Montagnani, Barnabé Ledoux, David Lacoste • 2026-03-14
Score: 13 climate policy, decarbonization
This paper investigates why global CO₂ emissions continue rising despite climate policies, hypothesizing that financial leverage creates structural barriers to decarbonization. Using a stochastic macro-financial model integrating credit dynamics, growth, and emissions, calibrated to multi-decade data from the US, China, France, and Denmark, the authors demonstrate that debt-financed growth systematically increases cumulative emissions by creating a "double constraint" where debt servicing requires sustained growth while growth remains energy-dependent. The key contribution is identifying a fundamental incompatibility between credit-driven growth regimes and emissions decoupling, suggesting that achieving net-zero targets requires restructuring credit allocation mechanisms rather than relying solely on efficiency improvements.
The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper examines why the US and China are accelerating AI investment despite catastrophic risks from AGI misalignment, contrary to standard real options theory which predicts delay under high uncertainty. Using a continuous-time preemption game with endogenous existential risk, the authors show that when catastrophic costs are shared globally, the risk term cancels out of equilibrium conditions, creating a "suicide region" where competitive pressures force rational actors to deploy AGI early despite negative risk-adjusted NPV. The key contribution is demonstrating that only internalizing ruin costs through private liability can restore option value and prevent premature deployment in winner-takes-all technology races with existential stakes.
