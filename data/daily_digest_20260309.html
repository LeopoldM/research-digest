The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper examines why the US and China are accelerating AI investment despite catastrophic AGI risks, contrary to standard real options theory that predicts delay under high uncertainty. Using a continuous-time preemption game with endogenous existential risk, the authors show that when catastrophic failure costs are globally shared, risk terms cancel out of equilibrium conditions, creating a "suicide region" where rational actors deploy AGI early despite negative risk-adjusted NPV. The key contribution is demonstrating that competitive dynamics can force premature deployment of potentially catastrophic technologies unless failure costs are internalized through private liability mechanisms that make safety research economically essential.
Existence of Equilibrium Mechanisms in Generalized Principal-Agent Problems with Interacting Teams
arxiv • Brian Roberson • 2026-02-23
Score: 9 mechanism design, principal-agent
This paper examines when equilibrium exists in multi-principal mechanism design games where principals simultaneously design incentive contracts for their teams in environments with strategic spillovers between teams. The authors develop a novel theoretical approach that tracks both truthful equilibrium outcomes and the sets of outcomes achievable through unilateral deviations to address discontinuities in incentive-compatible mechanism correspondences that can prevent equilibrium existence. The key contribution is establishing general sufficient conditions for equilibrium existence in these complex multi-principal settings, providing theoretical foundations for analyzing team production and multi-agency problems with strategic interactions.
A Real-Options-Aware Multi-Criteria Framework for Ex-Ante Real Estate Redevelopment Use Selection
arxiv • Roberto Garrone • 2026-01-15
Score: 9 screening, real options
This paper addresses the problem of selecting optimal future uses for underperforming real estate assets by developing a decision framework that accounts for irreversibility and uncertainty in redevelopment choices. The methodology combines real options theory with multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA) to evaluate redevelopment alternatives across dimensions of economic value, risk, complexity, and time-to-income while preserving managerial flexibility. The key contribution is operationalizing option value preservation through systematic ex-ante screening that reduces strategic misalignment and over-complexification in real estate redevelopment decisions.
Competition in Health Insurance Markets -- by Martin Gaynor, Amanda Starc
nber
Score: 9 adverse selection, market power
This paper examines how market concentration in U.S. health insurance markets affects competition and consumer welfare, with particular focus on how asymmetric information and adverse selection interact with insurer market power. Using empirical analysis, the authors document increasing concentration across commercial, Medicare Advantage, and Medicaid markets and demonstrate that insurer consolidation leads to higher premiums. The key contribution is showing how the interaction between market power and information asymmetries shapes pricing and plan design, while evaluating policy interventions (antitrust, risk adjustment, regulation) for improving competitive outcomes in these concentrated markets.
Delegation in Strategic Environments and Equilibrium Uniqueness
arxiv • Fedor Sandomirskiy, Ben Wincelberg • 2026-02-25
Score: 7 mechanism design
This paper investigates when games have unique equilibrium predictions under delegation arrangements where players coordinate through intermediaries. The methodology is theoretical, modeling delegation outcomes as coarse correlated equilibria (CCE) when intermediaries cannot punish deviations and as individually rational correlated profiles (IRCP) when they can. The key contribution is characterizing structural conditions for uniqueness in these delegation settings and establishing new uniqueness conditions for classical equilibria that don't require dominant strategies, with applications to robust mechanism design.
