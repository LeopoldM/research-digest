Fiscal Policy and Transition Risk -- by Stefano Carattini, Garth Heutel, Givi Melkadze, Inès Mourelon
nber
Score: 7 climate policy
This paper examines how climate policies interact with pre-existing distortionary taxes and financial frictions to create transition risk during the shift to a low-carbon economy. Using a dynamic stochastic general equilibrium model with financial frictions and existing labor/capital taxes, the authors simulate carbon taxes and abatement subsidies under various revenue recycling and financing scenarios. The key finding is that transition dynamics differ substantially from long-run equilibria and between policy instruments: carbon tax transition effects depend on financial frictions and revenue recycling choices, while abatement subsidies can generate contractionary dynamics due to distortionary financing, with macroprudential policy being more effective at mitigating transition risk under carbon taxation than subsidies.
