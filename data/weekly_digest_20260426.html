Mapping the causal structure of price formation in Texas's transitioning electricity market
arxiv • Shiva Madadkhani, Nils Sturma, Mathias Drton, Svetlana Ikonnikova • 2026-04-15
Score: 20 electricity market, wholesale electricity, renewable integration
This paper investigates how the causal structure of electricity price formation is evolving in Texas's wholesale market amid rapid renewable integration and demand growth. Using causal discovery methods applied to supply- and demand-side price determinants, the authors empirically analyze the shifting relationships between wind generation, natural gas prices, demand, and wholesale electricity prices across regions and time periods. The key finding is that wind generation has displaced natural gas as the dominant causal driver of day-ahead prices (with effects 3x larger than gas prices), while also creating complex spatial and temporal redistribution of congestion costs, challenging traditional models of gas-price-driven electricity markets.
Truthful Production Uncertainty in Electricity Markets: A Two-Stage Mechanism
arxiv • Shobhit Singhal, Lesia Mitridati, Licio Romao • 2026-04-02
Score: 13 market clearing, electricity market
This paper addresses how to incorporate production uncertainty from renewable generators into electricity market design to reduce total system costs. The methodology develops a two-stage mechanism extending Vickrey-Clarke-Groves (VCG) payments where producers report probability distributions of production forecasts in day-ahead markets, then actual production in real-time. The key contribution is proving this mechanism achieves truthful reporting of uncertainty (incentive compatibility) and individual rationality while reducing system-wide costs compared to current point-forecast approaches, validated through electricity market case studies.
Carbon Regulation and Competition in the European Airline Industry
arxiv • Ertian Chen, Lichao Chen, Lars Nesheim • 2026-03-29
Score: 13 carbon pricing, emissions trading
This paper examines how EU carbon pricing through the Emissions Trading System affects competition and welfare in the European airline industry. The authors employ a structural two-stage model of airline competition estimated on European market data, incorporating endogenous decisions on route entry, flight frequency, and pricing in counterfactual simulations. The key finding is that carbon regulation creates highly asymmetric impacts: consumer surplus falls up to 25% overall (90% in medium-haul markets), airline profits decline 8-45%, but short-haul markets experience net welfare gains as airlines reallocate capacity toward shorter routes, with carbon revenues ($0.9-3.1B) and avoided emissions benefits ($0.5-1.4B) partially offsetting welfare losses.
Lexicographic Robustness and the Efficiency of Optimal Mechanisms
arxiv • Ashwin Kambhampati • 2026-04-07
Score: 11 mechanism design, screening
This paper addresses how to design robust mechanisms when the planner is uncertain about the economic environment, proposing a lexicographic refinement of the standard maxmin criterion to overcome its lack of discriminatory power. Using theoretical mechanism design analysis, the authors characterize optimal mechanisms under their "proper robustness" criterion across different settings. The key finding is that lexicographic robustness yields ex post efficient outcomes in screening and auction environments, but identifies specific optimal inefficiencies in public good provision that worsen with economy size.
The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper investigates why the US and China are accelerating AI investment despite acknowledging catastrophic AGI risks, contrary to standard real options theory which predicts delay under extreme uncertainty. Using a continuous-time preemption game with endogenous existential risk, the authors show that when catastrophic failure costs (D) are globally shared rather than bounded by individual sunk costs (I), the risk terms cancel out of players' equilibrium conditions, creating a "suicide region" where rational agents deploy AGI early despite negative risk-adjusted NPV. The key contribution is demonstrating that competitive pressures override safety considerations in winner-takes-all races with systemic risks, and that only internalizing ruin costs through private liability can restore proper option value and incentivize safety research.
