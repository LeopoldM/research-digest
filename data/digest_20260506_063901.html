Mapping the causal structure of price formation in Texas's transitioning electricity market
arxiv • Shiva Madadkhani, Nils Sturma, Mathias Drton, Svetlana Ikonnikova • 2026-04-15
Score: 20 electricity market, wholesale electricity, renewable integration
This paper investigates how the causal structure of electricity price formation is evolving in Texas as renewable generation expands, using causal discovery methods applied to wholesale price data alongside supply and demand variables. The empirical analysis reveals that wind generation has become the dominant causal driver of day-ahead prices—with effects over 3 times larger than natural gas prices—fundamentally reshaping the market from its traditional gas-price-driven structure. The key contribution is demonstrating that renewable integration creates time-varying, region-specific causal relationships that redistribute congestion costs and require dynamic causal models for effective market planning and operation.
Principal-agent problems with adverse selection: A stochastic target problem formulation
arxiv • Guillermo Alonso Alvarez, Ibrahim Ekren, Liwei Huang • 2026-05-01
Score: 16 self-selection, screening, adverse selection
This paper investigates how a principal can optimally contract with an agent when the agent's cost type is unknown and only a single contract (rather than a menu) can be offered. The methodology combines stochastic target problem formulation with stochastic optimal control theory under partial information and state constraints to characterize both the agent's response and principal's optimization. The key contribution is showing that this constrained adverse selection problem can be solved via stochastic control methods, while also providing a framework to quantify the value of traditional screening contracts that allow self-selection.
Truthful Production Uncertainty in Electricity Markets: A Two-Stage Mechanism
arxiv • Shobhit Singhal, Lesia Mitridati, Licio Romao • 2026-04-02
Score: 13 market clearing, electricity market
This paper addresses how to efficiently incorporate production uncertainty from renewable sources into electricity market clearing by designing a mechanism that elicits truthful uncertainty information from generators. The methodology develops a theoretical two-stage mechanism extending Vickrey-Clarke-Groves (VCG) payments, where producers report probability distributions of their output in day-ahead markets rather than point forecasts, followed by actual production revelation in real-time. The key contribution is proving that this mechanism achieves incentive compatibility and individual rationality while reducing total system costs compared to current markets that ignore uncertainty information, as demonstrated through theoretical analysis and electricity market case studies.
MenuNet: A Strategy-Proof Mechanism for Matching Markets
arxiv • Zhaohong Sun, Makoto Yokoo • 2026-05-04
Score: 13 mechanism design, market clearing
This paper addresses how to design strategy-proof mechanisms for matching markets with complex distributional constraints where stable matchings may not exist. The methodology combines mechanism design theory with neural networks, proposing MenuNet—a framework that learns to generate personalized probabilistic menus and uses sequential choice rules to guarantee strategy-proofness by construction. The key contribution is demonstrating that MenuNet can effectively trade off between fairness (envy) and efficiency (waste) by outperforming Random Serial Dictatorship on envy measures and Deferred Acceptance on waste measures while maintaining computational scalability.
Strategic Bidding in 6G Spectrum Auctions with Large Language Models
arxiv • Ismail Lotfi, Ali Ghrayeb • 2026-04-27
Score: 13 mechanism design, bidding behavior
This paper investigates whether Large Language Models can effectively serve as strategic bidding agents in repeated 6G spectrum auctions for vehicular networks, where user equipment must optimize long-term utility under budget constraints. The methodology combines mechanism design theory (using VCG as a truthful benchmark) with computational experiments comparing LLM-guided bidding against truthful and heuristic strategies across repeated auction rounds. The key finding is that LLMs achieve near-equilibrium outcomes when theoretical assumptions hold, but more importantly, they outperform traditional approaches when assumptions break down (e.g., under budget constraints), demonstrating superior adaptability in approximating dynamic equilibria beyond static mechanism design predictions.
