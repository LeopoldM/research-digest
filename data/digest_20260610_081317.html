A Real-Options-Aware Multi-Criteria Framework for Ex-Ante Real Estate Redevelopment Use Selection
arxiv • Roberto Garrone • 2026-01-15
Score: 9 screening, real options
This study addresses the problem of selecting optimal redevelopment uses for underperforming real estate assets by developing a decision framework that accounts for irreversibility and uncertainty. The methodology combines real options theory with multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA) to evaluate redevelopment alternatives across dimensions of economic value, risk, complexity, and timing while preserving managerial flexibility. The key contribution is an integrated framework that treats redevelopment as strategic option selection rather than purely financial optimization, demonstrated to reduce misalignment and over-complexification in illustrative case applications.
Strategic Inertia and Institutional Change:A Behavioral Model of Price Reforms versus Action Deletion
arxiv • Madjid Eshaghi Gordji, Mohammadali Berahman, Hasti Eshaghi • 2026-05-20
Score: 7 climate policy
This paper examines why inefficient institutions persist despite superior alternatives by developing a quantal response equilibrium model with status-quo bias (QRE-SB) where players face fixed switching costs from inherited default actions. Using theoretical analysis in a binary coordination game framework, the authors compare price-based reforms (taxing the default action) versus regulatory bans (deleting the default action entirely). The key finding is that while price reforms only succeed above a specific threshold independent of bounded rationality, complete bans always induce transition to the superior equilibrium and yield strictly higher welfare than any finite tax when the superior outcome is Pareto-dominant.
Breaking Status-Quo Inertia in Living Temporal Games: Dynamic Intervention, Implementation, and Structural Design
arxiv • Madjid Eshaghi Gordji, Ali Jabbari, Mohammad Ali Berahman, Esmaiel Abounoori • 2026-05-18
Score: 7 mechanism design
This paper examines how a social planner can design dynamic interventions to overcome inefficient status-quo equilibria in continuous-time stochastic games where agents strategically control their activity states on temporal networks. The methodology combines game-theoretic analysis with mechanism design, proving structural results through uniformization techniques and constructing explicit mechanisms. The key contributions include: (1) a threshold theorem showing status-quo equilibria survive price interventions below a horizon-dependent critical bound, (2) a structural dominance result demonstrating that network modifications (replacing continuous-flow with discrete-transport edges) can eliminate inefficient equilibria when bounded transfers fail, and (3) an impossibility result for full implementation under private information, along with a second-best dynamic pivot mechanism.
Profit-Oriented Planning and Multi-Market Operation Model for Hybrid Energy Storage Systems
arxiv • Lizhong Zhang, Junqi Liu, Jianxiao Wang, Lei Zhu • 2026-05-18
Score: 7 market clearing
This paper addresses how an independent energy storage operator (ESO) can jointly optimize capacity planning and multi-market bidding strategies for hybrid energy storage systems (HESS) while accounting for market power effects. The methodology employs a bi-level optimization framework where the upper level determines optimal capacities and bidding strategies across day-ahead energy-reserve and real-time balancing markets, while the lower level represents market clearing, solved via mixed-integer linear programming with Benders' decomposition. The key finding is that profit-maximizing ESOs strategically allocate heterogeneous storage technologies based on power-to-capacity ratios—high-ratio systems for energy arbitrage and low-ratio systems for reserve provision—with potential internal power transfers under grid constraints.
Supply Chain Coordination Mechanism Design: Consensus Planning Protocol Meets Vickrey-Clarke-Groves Mechanism
arxiv • Dirk Bergemann, Garrett van Ryzin, Jiaxuan Li • 2026-05-15
Score: 7 mechanism design
This paper addresses how to design truthful coordination mechanisms for supply chain collaboration between retailers and vendors to minimize joint Cost to Serve. The authors develop a theoretical framework that integrates Vickrey-Clarke-Groves (VCG) mechanisms with Consensus Planning Protocol (CPP), proving that this combination maintains dominant-strategy incentive compatibility and efficiency in high-dimensional settings. The key contribution is demonstrating that the CPP-VCG framework can achieve truthful, efficient coordination in distributed supply chain optimization while introducing activity fees to improve revenue properties for the retailer.
