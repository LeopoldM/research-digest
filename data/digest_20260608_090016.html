A Real-Options-Aware Multi-Criteria Framework for Ex-Ante Real Estate Redevelopment Use Selection
arxiv • Roberto Garrone • 2026-01-15
Score: 9 screening, real options
This study addresses how to optimally select the intended use for underperforming real estate assets requiring redevelopment, framing this as a strategic option selection problem rather than a design or financial optimization issue. The methodology combines real options theory (incorporating irreversibility and managerial flexibility) with multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA) to evaluate alternatives across dimensions of economic value, risk, complexity, and time-to-income. The key contribution is a decision framework that preserves option value through systematic ex-ante screening, demonstrated to reduce over-complexification and use-demand misalignment across different property types and urban contexts.
Strategic Inertia and Institutional Change:A Behavioral Model of Price Reforms versus Action Deletion
arxiv • Madjid Eshaghi Gordji, Mohammadali Berahman, Hasti Eshaghi • 2026-05-20
Score: 7 climate policy
This paper investigates why inefficient institutions persist despite available superior alternatives by developing a quantal response equilibrium model with status-quo bias (QRE-SB) where players face fixed switching costs from inherited default actions. Using theoretical analysis in a binary coordination game framework, the authors compare price-based reforms (taxing the inefficient default) versus regulatory bans (eliminating the default entirely). The key finding is that while price reforms only succeed above a critical tax threshold (independent of bounded rationality levels), bans always force transitions to superior equilibria and yield strictly higher welfare than any finite tax when the superior outcome is Pareto-dominant, providing formal justification for "ban rather than tax" policies in contexts like climate regulation.
Breaking Status-Quo Inertia in Living Temporal Games: Dynamic Intervention, Implementation, and Structural Design
arxiv • Madjid Eshaghi Gordji, Ali Jabbari, Mohammad Ali Berahman, Esmaiel Abounoori • 2026-05-18
Score: 7 mechanism design
This paper studies how a planner can design dynamic interventions to overcome inefficient status-quo equilibria in continuous-time stochastic games where agents control their temporal network states. Using theoretical analysis, the authors prove a threshold theorem showing that price-based transfers below a critical bound cannot eliminate status-quo inertia, and demonstrate structural dominance results where single edge modifications (switching from continuous-flow to discrete-transport) can succeed where bounded transfer interventions fail. For private information settings, they establish an impossibility theorem for first-best implementation under standard mechanism design constraints, but construct a dynamic pivot mechanism achieving second-best efficiency, while showing that discrete-transport network structures weakly expand implementable outcomes relative to continuous-flow structures.
Profit-Oriented Planning and Multi-Market Operation Model for Hybrid Energy Storage Systems
arxiv • Lizhong Zhang, Junqi Liu, Jianxiao Wang, Lei Zhu • 2026-05-18
Score: 7 market clearing
This paper investigates the joint optimization of capacity planning and multi-market bidding strategies for profit-maximizing energy storage operators (ESOs) using hybrid energy storage systems (HESS) that can influence market prices. The authors develop a bi-level optimization framework where the upper level determines optimal capacities and bidding strategies across day-ahead energy-reserve and real-time balancing markets, while the lower level represents market clearing, solved via mixed-integer linear programming with Benders' decomposition. The key finding is that ESOs can strategically allocate heterogeneous storage technologies based on their power-to-capacity ratios—high-ratio systems for energy arbitrage and low-ratio systems for reserve provision—with potential internal power transfers when grid constraints exist.
Supply Chain Coordination Mechanism Design: Consensus Planning Protocol Meets Vickrey-Clarke-Groves Mechanism
arxiv • Dirk Bergemann, Garrett van Ryzin, Jiaxuan Li • 2026-05-15
Score: 7 mechanism design
This paper investigates how to design truthful and efficient coordination mechanisms for retailer-vendor supply chain collaboration to minimize joint Cost to Serve. The authors develop a theoretical framework combining the Vickrey-Clarke-Groves (VCG) auction mechanism with a Consensus Planning Protocol (CPP) for distributed optimization in high-dimensional settings. The key contribution is proving that this CPP-VCG integration maintains dominant-strategy incentive compatibility and efficiency while introducing an activity fee structure that enhances revenue properties for the retailer without compromising the mechanism's desirable theoretical properties.
