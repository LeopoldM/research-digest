The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper examines why the US and China are accelerating AGI development despite acknowledging catastrophic risks, which contradicts standard real options theory that predicts investment delay under extreme uncertainty. The authors develop a continuous-time preemption game model where the cost of AGI failure is represented by a globally-shared systemic ruin parameter that cancels out of players' equilibrium conditions due to its symmetric impact. The key finding is that competitive dynamics create a "suicide region" where rational actors deploy AGI systems early despite negative risk-adjusted returns, and this race can only be stopped by internalizing ruin costs through private liability mechanisms that make safety research economically necessary.
