The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper investigates why the US and China are accelerating AI investment despite catastrophic risks from AGI misalignment, contrary to standard real options theory that predicts delay under extreme uncertainty. The authors develop a continuous-time preemption game model with endogenous existential risk, where the cost of failure includes a global systemic ruin parameter that cancels out of equilibrium conditions due to shared risk exposure. The key finding is that competitive pressures create a "suicide region" where rational actors deploy AGI systems early despite negative risk-adjusted NPV, and only internalizing ruin costs through private liability can restore optimal delay incentives.
Existence of Equilibrium Mechanisms in Generalized Principal-Agent Problems with Interacting Teams
arxiv • Brian Roberson • 2026-02-23
Score: 9 mechanism design, principal-agent
This paper investigates when equilibrium exists in multi-principal mechanism design games where teams' incentive structures create strategic spillovers between principals. The authors develop a novel theoretical approach that simultaneously tracks truthful outcome distributions and deviation sets to address discontinuities in incentive-compatible mechanism correspondences that can prevent equilibrium existence (as in Myerson 1982). The key contribution is establishing general sufficient conditions for equilibrium existence in these complex environments, providing theoretical foundations for analyzing team production and multi-agency problems with interacting principals.
A Real-Options-Aware Multi-Criteria Framework for Ex-Ante Real Estate Redevelopment Use Selection
arxiv • Roberto Garrone • 2026-01-15
Score: 9 screening, real options
This paper addresses how to systematically select optimal future uses for underperforming real estate assets requiring redevelopment. The methodology combines real options theory with multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA) to create a framework that evaluates potential uses across dimensions of economic value, risk, complexity, and timing while preserving managerial flexibility. The key contribution is operationalizing option value preservation through structured ex-ante screening that treats redevelopment as strategic option selection rather than purely financial optimization, demonstrating reduced complexity and better use-demand alignment across diverse asset types.
Delegation in Strategic Environments and Equilibrium Uniqueness
arxiv • Fedor Sandomirskiy, Ben Wincelberg • 2026-02-25
Score: 7 mechanism design
This paper investigates when strategic games have unique equilibrium predictions under delegation to intermediaries who may facilitate coordination among players. The methodology is theoretical, modeling delegation outcomes through coarse correlated equilibria (CCE) when intermediaries cannot punish deviations and individually rational correlated profiles (IRCP) when they can, then characterizing conditions for uniqueness in each case. The key contribution is establishing structural conditions for equilibrium uniqueness that extend beyond dominant strategy environments, providing robust predictions across various information structures and dynamics, with applications to designing collusion-proof mechanisms.
Robust Mechanism Design with Anonymous Information
arxiv • Zhihao Gavin Tang, Shixin Wang • 2026-02-24
Score: 7 mechanism design
This paper studies robust auction design when sellers only observe anonymous, aggregated statistics (like highest or lowest bids) rather than complete bidder information, asking how to maximize worst-case expected revenue across all possible underlying bidder distributions consistent with these limited observations. Using theoretical mechanism design analysis, the authors prove that simple, widely-used mechanisms are robustly optimal: posted prices when observing highest values, Myerson auctions when observing lowest values, and second-price auctions with reserves for intermediate order statistics. The key contribution demonstrates that standard mechanisms designed for i.i.d. distributions achieve worst-case optimality under limited information, providing theoretical justification for simple auction formats while naturally ensuring fairness and privacy through information constraints rather than explicit regulatory requirements.
