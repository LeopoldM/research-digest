Information Design and Mechanism Design: An Integrated Framework
arxiv • Dirk Bergemann, Tibor Heumann, Stephen Morris • 2026-01-24
Score: 18 mechanism design, auction theory, screening
This paper investigates how to jointly optimize mechanism design and information design in screening environments with quasilinear utility. The methodology is theoretical, employing majorization theory and quantile functions to show that both problems reduce to maximizing linear functionals subject to majorization constraints. The key contribution is demonstrating that when a designer simultaneously chooses mechanisms and information structures, the optimal solution involves pooling of values and allocations, unifying and extending classic auction theory results while providing new insights into welfare effects of joint optimization.
The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper examines why the US and China are accelerating AGI development despite acknowledging catastrophic risks, contradicting standard real options theory that predicts investment delay under high uncertainty. The authors develop a continuous-time preemption game model where competitive pressures between rational actors create a "suicide region" - an equilibrium where shared existential risk cancels out of players' optimization conditions, forcing early deployment despite negative risk-adjusted returns. The key contribution shows that competitive dynamics can lead rational agents to pursue globally destructive strategies, and that only internalizing ruin costs through private liability can restore socially optimal delay incentives.
Dynamic Mechanism Design without Monetary Transfers: A Queueing Theory Approach
arxiv • Zihao Li, Xuandong Chen • 2026-01-28
Score: 9 mechanism design, optimal mechanism
This paper examines how to optimally allocate stochastically arriving agents and goods when monetary transfers are unavailable but costly type verification is possible. Using a queueing-theoretic framework, the authors characterize the steady-state optimal mechanism as a dynamic threshold policy where admission and allocation thresholds depend on current queue states. The key contribution is demonstrating that state-dependent thresholds can achieve optimality in dynamic allocation problems without monetary instruments, with applications to public housing, grants, and internal capital allocation.
The Algorithmic Barrier: Quantifying Artificial Frictional Unemployment in Automated Recruitment Systems
arxiv • Ibrahim Denis Fofanah • 2026-01-20
Score: 9 screening, information asymmetry
This paper investigates whether automated recruitment systems using keyword-based screening artificially increase frictional unemployment by creating semantic mismatches between job seekers and vacancies. The authors employ controlled simulations comparing traditional keyword screening with semantic matching using high-dimensional vector representations of resumes and job descriptions. The key finding is that semantic matching significantly improves recall and matching efficiency without sacrificing precision, suggesting that outdated algorithmic hiring systems impose substantial economic costs on labor market efficiency.
A Real-Options-Aware Multi-Criteria Framework for Ex-Ante Real Estate Redevelopment Use Selection
arxiv • Roberto Garrone • 2026-01-15
Score: 9 screening, real options
This paper addresses how to select optimal intended uses for underperforming real estate assets requiring redevelopment by developing a decision framework that treats use selection as a strategic options problem rather than purely financial optimization. The methodology combines real options theory (focusing on irreversibility and managerial flexibility) with multi-criteria decision analysis (MCDA) to evaluate alternatives across economic value, risk, complexity, and time-to-income dimensions. The key contribution is an integrated analytical framework that preserves option value through systematic ex-ante screening, demonstrated to reduce over-complexification and use-demand misalignment across different asset types and urban contexts.
