Principal-agent problems with adverse selection: A stochastic target problem formulation
arxiv • Guillermo Alonso Alvarez, Ibrahim Ekren, Liwei Huang • 2026-05-01
Score: 16 self-selection, screening, adverse selection
This paper examines how a principal should design optimal contracts under adverse selection when restricted to offering a single contract rather than a menu of self-selecting options. The methodology combines stochastic control theory with mechanism design, reformulating the agent's problem as a stochastic target problem and the principal's problem as optimal control under partial information with state constraints. The key contribution is characterizing the "credible domain" of feasible contracts and demonstrating that this framework can also quantify the value of traditional screening mechanisms relative to the single-contract constraint.
A Truthful Multiunit Profit-Optimal Mechanism for Synthesizing Social Laws
arxiv • Jun Wu, Jian Huang, Chongjun Wang • 2026-05-16
Score: 16 mechanism design, procurement auction, optimal mechanism
This paper addresses Social Law Synthesis (SLS) in multi-agent systems by formulating it as a Bayesian single-parameter procurement auction where agents strategically bid on synthesized social laws expressed in Alternating-time Temporal Logic (ATL). The methodology combines mechanism design theory with computational complexity analysis, proving that allocation determination is FP^NP-complete and developing polynomial-time reduction techniques that encode ATL semantics into integer linear programming constraints. The key contribution is the PO-ASL mechanism, which achieves truthfulness, individual rationality, and profit-optimality while making the computationally challenging multi-unit mechanism design problem tractable through standard ILP solvers.
