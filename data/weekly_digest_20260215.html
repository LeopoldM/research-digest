Information Design and Mechanism Design: An Integrated Framework
arxiv • Dirk Bergemann, Tibor Heumann, Stephen Morris • 2026-01-24
Score: 18 mechanism design, auction theory, screening
This paper investigates how to optimally design both information structures and allocation mechanisms simultaneously in screening environments with quasilinear utility. The methodology is theoretical, using majorization theory and quantile functions to show that both information design and mechanism design problems can be formulated as linear optimization subject to majorization constraints. The key contribution is proving that when jointly optimizing mechanisms and information structures, pooling of agent types and their associated allocations is always optimal, and this unified framework extends classical auction theory results while providing new welfare insights for joint optimization problems.
The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper examines why the US and China are accelerating AI investment despite catastrophic AGI risks, contrary to standard real options theory which predicts delay under high uncertainty. Using a continuous-time preemption game with endogenous existential risk, the authors show that when catastrophic costs are shared globally, risk terms cancel out of equilibrium conditions, creating a "suicide region" where competitive pressures force early AGI deployment despite negative risk-adjusted returns. The key contribution is demonstrating that only internalizing ruin costs through private liability can restore option value and incentivize safety research as economically viable.
