AI in Charge: Large-Scale Experimental Evidence on Electric Vehicle Charging Demand -- by Robert D. Metcalfe, Andrew Schein, Cohen R. Simpson, Yixin Sun
nber
Score: 27 wholesale electricity, retail electricity, electricity pricing
This paper investigates whether AI-managed dynamic pricing can effectively shift electric vehicle charging demand to align with low-carbon electricity supply and reduce system costs. The researchers conducted a large-scale randomized field experiment across multiple countries (UK, US, Germany, Spain) with financial incentives for EV tariff enrollment, analyzing charging behavior and demand patterns over more than a year. They find that the AI-managed tariff achieved a 42% reduction in peak-hour household electricity demand with complete load shifting to lower-cost/lower-carbon periods, generated substantial consumer savings, and demonstrated low AI override rates, suggesting superior efficiency compared to real-time pricing without AI coordination.
