Information Design and Mechanism Design: An Integrated Framework
arxiv • Dirk Bergemann, Tibor Heumann, Stephen Morris • 2026-01-24
Score: 18 mechanism design, auction theory, screening
This paper investigates how to optimally design both mechanisms (allocation rules) and information structures simultaneously in screening environments. Using majorization theory and quantile functions, the authors show that both problems reduce to maximizing linear functionals subject to majorization constraints—mechanism design chooses allocations majorized by inventory, while information design chooses structures majorized by priors. The key finding is that joint optimization leads to a bilinear problem where pooling of values and allocations is always optimal, unifying auction theory with information design and revealing new insights about welfare effects of simultaneous optimization.
The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper examines why the US and China are accelerating AI investment despite acknowledging catastrophic risks from AGI misalignment, contrary to standard real options theory which predicts delay under high uncertainty. Using a continuous-time preemption game with endogenous existential risk, the authors show that when failure costs include a systemic ruin parameter (D) shared globally rather than just sunk investment costs, competitive pressures create a "suicide region" where rational agents deploy AGI early despite negative risk-adjusted NPV because the shared risk term cancels out of equilibrium conditions. The key contribution is demonstrating that only internalizing ruin costs through private liability can restore option value and halt the race, as "warning shots" fail to deter acceleration in winner-takes-all competition.
