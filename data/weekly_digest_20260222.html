Information Design and Mechanism Design: An Integrated Framework
arxiv • Dirk Bergemann, Tibor Heumann, Stephen Morris • 2026-01-24
Score: 18 mechanism design, auction theory, screening
This paper examines how a designer can jointly optimize mechanism design (allocations) and information design (information structures) in screening environments with quasilinear utility. Using majorization theory and quantile functions, the authors show that both problems reduce to maximizing linear functionals subject to majorization constraints—mechanisms choose allocations majorized by inventory, while information design chooses structures majorized by the prior. The key finding is that when jointly optimizing both dimensions, pooling of values and allocations is always optimal, unifying classical auction theory with information design and revealing new insights about welfare effects.
The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper examines why the US and China are accelerating AGI development despite acknowledging catastrophic risks, contrary to standard real options theory which predicts investment delay under high uncertainty. The authors develop a continuous-time preemption game model where both players face a shared existential ruin parameter that scales with development speed, causing the risk terms to cancel out in equilibrium conditions. The key finding is that competitive dynamics create a "suicide region" where rational actors deploy AGI early despite negative risk-adjusted returns, and only internalizing ruin costs through private liability can restore safety incentives and halt the dangerous race.
