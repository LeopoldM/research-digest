Truthful Production Uncertainty in Electricity Markets: A Two-Stage Mechanism
arxiv • Shobhit Singhal, Lesia Mitridati, Licio Romao • 2026-04-02
Score: 13 market clearing, electricity market
This paper addresses how to incorporate production uncertainty from renewable sources into electricity market design to reduce total system costs. The methodology develops a theoretical two-stage mechanism where producers report probability distributions of their expected output in day-ahead markets (rather than point forecasts), with payments based on an extension of Vickrey-Clarke-Groves (VCG) auctions, validated through electricity market simulations. The key contribution is proving this mechanism achieves truthful reporting of uncertainty and individual rationality while reducing system-wide balancing costs compared to current point-forecast-based day-ahead markets.
Carbon Regulation and Competition in the European Airline Industry
arxiv • Ertian Chen, Lichao Chen, Lars Nesheim • 2026-03-29
Score: 13 carbon pricing, emissions trading
This paper examines how EU Emissions Trading System carbon pricing affects competition and welfare in the European airline industry. The authors use a structural two-stage model of airline competition estimated on European market share and pricing data, incorporating endogenous route entry, flight frequency, and pricing decisions. The key finding is that carbon regulation creates highly asymmetric impacts: consumer surplus falls up to 25% overall (90% in medium-haul markets), while short-haul markets experience net welfare gains as airlines reallocate capacity toward shorter routes, with carbon revenues ($0.9-3.1B) and avoided emissions benefits ($0.5-1.4B) partially offsetting welfare losses.
Lexicographic Robustness and the Efficiency of Optimal Mechanisms
arxiv • Ashwin Kambhampati • 2026-04-07
Score: 11 mechanism design, screening
This paper addresses how to select robust mechanisms when there is uncertainty about the economic environment, proposing lexicographic refinements of the standard maxmin criterion to narrow down the typically large set of maxmin-optimal mechanisms. The methodology is theoretical, developing the concept of "proper robustness" as the strongest lexicographic refinement and characterizing optimal mechanisms in screening, auction, and public goods settings. The key finding is that proper robustness yields ex post efficient mechanisms in screening and auction environments, but in public goods provision it identifies specific optimal inefficiencies that become severe as economy size increases.
The Suicide Region: Option Games and the Race to Artificial General Intelligence
arxiv • David Tan • 2025-12-08
Score: 11 mechanism design, real options
This paper examines why the US and China are accelerating AGI development despite catastrophic risks, contrary to standard real options theory that predicts delay under high uncertainty. Using a continuous-time preemption game with endogenous existential risk, the authors model how shared global catastrophic costs (parameter D) mathematically cancel out of players' equilibrium conditions, creating a "suicide region" where competitive pressures drive premature deployment despite negative risk-adjusted NPV. The key contribution is showing that only internalizing ruin costs through private liability can restore option value and incentivize delay, as "warning shots" fail to deter acceleration in winner-takes-all competition.
Personalization as a Game: Equilibrium-Guided Generative Modeling for Physician Behavior in Pharmaceutical Engagement
arxiv • Suyash Mishra • 2026-04-08
Score: 9 mechanism design, game theory
This paper develops EGPF, a framework that models pharmaceutical-physician interactions as incomplete-information Bayesian games to optimize personalized physician engagement strategies. The methodology combines game-theoretic modeling (where physician behavioral types are inferred and equilibrium strategies guide LLM-based content generation) with category theory for composable behavior representation and information theory for adaptive feedback mechanisms. The key contribution is achieving a 34% improvement in engagement while providing theoretical guarantees including O(K log K / (t·C_min)) convergence rates and finite-sample regret bounds, with novel extensions for privacy-personalization tradeoffs and population-level dynamics.
